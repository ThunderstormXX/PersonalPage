Igor Ignashin

Research Lead, ML and Optimization

I am an M.Sc. student at MIPT, Faculty of Applied Mathematics and Informatics, Department of Intelligent Data Analysis.

My work is centered on machine learning optimization, stochastic dynamics of SGD, LLM compression and inference acceleration, minimax problems, and applied traffic assignment.

I work as a Research Lead in BRAIn Lab / MIRAI under the supervision of Alexander Beznosikov, collaborate with Demyan Yarmoshik from LAB MMO in Alexander Gasnikov's research group, and recently visited MBZUAI as a research student under Eduard Gorbunov.

I also work on SGD analysis and multi-agent reinforcement learning with Andrei Leonidov's team.

Research Focus

01

Optimization Theory

Convergence analysis for minimax and LMO-based methods, including Frank-Wolfe variants and practical deep learning optimizers.

02

Stochastic Dynamics

Experiments and theory for finite-step SGD behavior beyond Brownian-motion approximations and standard Langevin models.

03

LLM Efficiency

Student research projects and experiments on pruning, distillation, early exiting, multi-agent RL, and LLM training dynamics.
